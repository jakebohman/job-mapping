Where hiring breaks from the national pattern

Ten of the largest U.S. metros, ranked by how far each one's mix of open jobs departs from the national norm. Postings are counted by sector — a near-census — so the list reflects what employers advertise, where, not who ranks highest in a search. The list does the looking.

How to read this

Each bar measures a sector's share of a metro's postings against its national share, on a log scale — a bar reaching the same length on both sides means twice, or half, the national rate. Healthcare is the largest sector everywhere, so it rarely appears here: the index surfaces what is distinctive about a metro, not what is merely large.
